There's a particular kind of frustration I hear from experienced leaders. They did everything right. They earned their position, they know the work cold, and they lead the way they were led. And it isn't working anymore, especially with younger employees.

The instinct is to blame the generation. The more useful move is to notice that authority itself changed shape.

This is the first in a series on leading people who don't respond to the old levers, written for the managers expected to figure it out without a map.
